Pong definitions and rules:

1.) Canvas:

    -) Background:  black
    -) Players:     white
    -) Ball:        white
    -) U/L Borders: white
    -) Points:      white
    -) Middle line: white
    -) Timer:       white / red

2.) Players:

    -) Will be displayed via rectangles on the left and right halves of the screen. There may be space between the left/right screen borders and the players.
    -) The player on the left side will be considered player 1 and the one on the right side will be considered player 2.
    -) Can ONLY move vertically between the upper and lower borders.
    -) When playing locally against an opponent, moving will be done via keys 'W' or 'ARROW_UP' for moving up and 'S' or 'ARROW_DOWN' for moving down for player 1 and player 2 respectively.
    -) When playing against an AI opponent, moving will be done via keys 'W' for moving up and 'S' for moving down.

3.) Ball:

    -) EVERY time a new round starts, meaning until one opponent scores a point, the ball will RANDOMLY choose a direction and a RANDOM angle to go from the middle line.
    -) The angle should be within reasonable boundaries
    -) After the round starts, the ball should have a slower intial speed. When the ball is then returned by a player, the velocity should increase to a reasonable tempo.
    -) Apart from the inital velocity to the first return of the ball, its speed should consistently be the same.
    -) If a player touches the ball from their front or the the top/bottom of the rectangle, the direction should change according on where the ball hit the player.

4.) Match:

    -) The game ends either when one player reaches the maximum number of points or when the match duration is exceeded.
    -) Should both players have the sam amount of point when the match duration is exceeded, the match prolongs until one player scores a point.
    -) A player earns points by scoring a goal against the opponents by getting the ball past their enemies.
    -) There should be a timer showing the remaining time of the match. Before the match starts, there should be a previous countdown of at least three seconds.
    -) When the match ends, a message should be displayed showing the winner and the final score.
